# Team

## Principal Investigator

### Dr Isabel Berwian (PI)

Isabel Berwian is Associate Professor of Interventional Methods in Clinical Psychology at University College London. Her research focuses on understanding how interventions for depression and anxiety work and how they can be improved, combining computational modelling, experimental approaches, clinical research, and neuroimaging.

She completed her PhD at the Translational Neuromodeling Unit in Zurich and trained as a psychotherapist in Germany. During her postdoctoral work at Princeton University, she developed her research programme on modelling psychological interventions using computational frameworks.

## Lab Members

### Emma Leger (MSc Student)

Emma Leger is a Neuroscience MSc student at University College London, where she is completing her dissertation in Dr Isabel Berwian’s lab. Before UCL, she read Neuroscience at the University of St Andrews, where her interest in computational approaches to the brain first developed.

Her research takes a computational approach to the mechanisms underlying the spontaneous return of fear, motivated by a broader interest in PTSD, particularly in supporting veterans. For her dissertation, she is testing whether the selective maintenance of aversive memories drives the return of fear, using emotional facial stimuli to examine whether more aversive content produces a stronger return. The work is currently being conducted in the general population, with plans to extend it to symptom-based samples in the future.

## Affiliated Members

### Jialing Ding (Research Assistant)

Jialing Ding is interested in learning and decision-making processes in individuals with psychiatric disorders, with a focus on how individuals perceive the world and the factors underlying their behaviour.

In the lab, Jialing is working on a longitudinal clinical study to identify behavioural markers that predict treatment responses to psychotherapies and the future development of psychiatric disorders.

---

### Dr Jamie Chiu (PhD Student)

Jamie Chiu aims to develop effective, personalized non-pharmaceutical treatments for depression and anxiety. Motivated by clinical experience as a therapist, the research focuses on understanding which treatments work best for whom.

Jamie is pursuing a PhD in Psychology and Neuroscience at Princeton University, with additional training in Statistics and Machine Learning. The research focuses on modelling readiness for change to improve matching individuals to effective interventions.

Jamie is also the founder of The Brightly Project, which develops digital tools to connect at-risk students with supportive adults in school settings. Outside of research, Jamie enjoys hiking, reading, and photography.
