# Computational Psychotherapy Lab

How do interventions for affective disorders lead to change, how they be improved and how can we determine which intervention a specific individual will benefit from?

These are the key questions we address in the Computational Psychotherapy Lab at UCL. We are an interdisciplinary lab led by Dr. Isabel Berwian. 

---

## Research

To approach the research questions we model psychological interventions as processes of learning and memory. We formalise these processes using computational frameworks such as reinforcement learning and Bayesian inference, allowing us to derive precise, testable hypotheses about how interventions operate and how they can be improved. 

We then develop behavioural tasks and computational models that capture learning, memory, and decision-making processes, and apply them in longitudinal clinical intervention studies to test the hypotheses. This approach enables us to identify mechanisms of change and develop predictors of treatment response that can inform the selection of interventions.

Our work spans the full pipeline from task and model development to clinical trials. In the long term, we aim to establish a mechanistic, learning-based framework for understanding how interventions produce lasting change and how they can be optimally combined and sequenced.
